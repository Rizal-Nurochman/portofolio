# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Prospective clients and hiring managers evaluating a creative, multidisciplinary maker. They arrive curious but time-pressed, often from a link shared on social or in a message, and they want to quickly get a feel for the person behind the work before deciding whether to reach out. The site speaks to people making a hiring or collaboration decision, so the work needs to land fast and the personality needs to come through just as fast.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that shows a solid body of creative, multidisciplinary work (roughly 7–15 pieces) and turns interested visitors into conversations. Success is a visitor who leaves feeling they know who this person is and reaches out to hire or collaborate.

## Conversion & proof
- Primary and secondary CTA: Primary is "reach out to hire me" via email; secondary is connecting through social profiles for visitors who want to follow along before committing.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: A distinct sense of the person — playful, expressive, warm — paired with proof they make good work.
- Belief ladder: (1) This person is real and has genuine range. (2) The work is good and varied. (3) I like their personality and would enjoy working with them. (4) Reaching out is easy and low-friction.
- Proof on hand: The work itself (7–15 pieces). No testimonials, press, or client logos supplied yet — add any to `.impeccable/assets/proof/` when available.

## Brand Personality

Playful and expressive, warm and personal. The voice is a real human talking, not a studio brochure — inviting, a little bit of fun, confident without posturing. The site should feel like meeting the person, with color, motion, and typography carrying that character rather than sitting quietly out of the way.

## Anti-references

Not generic SaaS or startup landing pages. Not cold minimalism — warmth and personality must come through. Not overdesigned or busy — expressive does not mean cluttered; there is still room to breathe. Not dark, techy, or neon.

## Design Principles

Personality first — the design should feel like the person, not a template. Show, don't tell — let the work and the voice do the convincing rather than adjectives. Warm without clutter — expressive energy balanced with breathing room so it never tips into busy. Make reaching out effortless — the path to contact is always close and obvious. Earn the reach-out — carry the visitor up the belief ladder before asking.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text contrast ≥ 4.5:1, meaningful focus states, keyboard-navigable throughout, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation given the expressive, motion-forward direction.
